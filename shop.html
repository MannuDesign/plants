Indoor plants have gained popularity not only for being low-effort house companions but also for being stunning additions to all kinds of interior aesthetics. With wonderful green foliage and occasional blooms depending on which indoor plant you choose, these green housemates can also grace your space with more than just aesthetics.

We?ve already established that indoor plants have a charm and grace that no other piece of home decor can parallel. But what are the other benefits of keeping plants in your living or office spaces indoors?

Indoor house plants help purify the air in their surroundings by absorbing harmful gases such as benzene, formaldehyde, and carbon monoxide. Additionally, they also release more oxygen, meaning they remove harmful substances and add useful, beneficial ones.

Through a process called transpiration, indoor plants release moisture through their leaves. This moisture release can lead to an increase in humidity levels in indoor spaces. If you live in relatively dry areas or even if your indoor spaces tend to become drier, plants can help with that!

There are a few indoor home plants that often tend to have sound-absorbing qualities. This allows them to act as natural sound absorbers that reduce noise in indoor spaces and create a more peaceful living or working environment.

It has been proven through studies that indoor plants have certain positive effects on mental health. If you are a regular when it comes to staying indoors and don?t often have too much contact with nature, plants will help you bring a slice of nature indoors. Plants reduce stress, anxiety, and symptoms of depression. Simply caring for plants and being around them can promote feelings of calmness and well-being.

Research suggests that indoor plants have regularly proven to promote faster healing and recovery times among patients in hospitals and healthcare facilities. The presence of plants has been linked to reduced pain, lower blood pressure, and shorter hospital stays. This also applies for even those times when you are at home and recovering from a simple fever!

Surrounding yourself with plants tends to stimulate creativity and innovation. Indoor plants can inspire new ideas and perspectives, making them valuable additions to creative workspaces such as offices, studios, and classrooms.
